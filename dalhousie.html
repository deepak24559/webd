How To Reach??

  • By Air: To reach Dalhousie by air, the nearest airport is Gaggal Airport (also known as Kangra Airport), which is located approximately 130 km from Dalhousie
  • By Rail: To reach Dalhousie by train, the nearest major railway station is Pathankot Junction, located approximately 80 km from Dalhousie
  • By Road: Reaching Dalhousie by road is a scenic and enjoyable journey, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys

Main tourist places!!

  • Khajjiar
    • Often referred to as the "Mini Switzerland of India," Khajjiar is a beautiful meadow surrounded by forests and snow-capped peaks. It offers opportunities for picnics, paragliding, and nature walks.
  • Panchpula
    • Panchpula is a scenic spot with a waterfall, located about 3 km from Dalhousie. It’s a popular spot for a picnic and offers a serene setting with picturesque views.
  • Dainkund Peak
    • Dainkund is the highest point in Dalhousie, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, valleys, and the beautiful Khajjiar meadow. The peak is also known for its lush forests and the "Pholani Devi Temple."
  • St. John's Church
    • Built in 1863, St. John’s Church is one of the oldest churches in Dalhousie and a perfect example of colonial architecture. Its peaceful setting and historical significance make it a must-visit.
  • St. Andrew's Church
    • Another colonial-era church, St. Andrew's Church is famous for its stunning architecture, quaint interiors, and panoramic views of the town.

Fair and Fests

  • . Chaitti MelaChaitti Mela is one of the major fairs in the region, attracting a large number of locals and tourists. It celebrates the religious and cultural significance of the region with performances of folk dances, music, and traditional rituals. The fair is a vibrant celebration with stalls selling local handicrafts, clothes, and other items.
  • Minjar Mela Minjar Mela is one of the oldest and most popular fairs in the Chamba district. It celebrates the harvest season and is dedicated to the local deity, Lord Raghuvir. The mela features traditional dances, music, and processions, along with a colorful market selling local goods. The "Minjar," a golden tassel, is carried around in the procession, symbolizing prosperity and well-being..
  • Baisakhi Festival Baisakhi is celebrated as a harvest festival in Punjab and Himachal Pradesh. In Dalhousie, the festival is marked by traditional dances like Bhangra and Gidda, local fairs, and prayers. It’s a time when locals come together to celebrate the end of the harvest season..
  • Shivratri FestivalShivratri is a major religious festival celebrated with much enthusiasm in Dalhousie. Devotees visit temples, perform rituals, and offer prayers to Lord Shiva. There are local fairs and processions, along with religious ceremonies..
  • Tibetan New Year (Losar Festival)The Tibetan New Year, also known as Losar, is an important festival for the Tibetan population in Dalhousie. It is celebrated with traditional Tibetan dances, music, and feasts. The festivities are marked by prayers for peace, prosperity, and good health.

Weather

Dalhousie experiences a pleasant climate year-round, with cool summers (15°C to 30°C) perfect for sightseeing. The monsoon season (July to September) brings moderate rainfall and lush greenery, though landslides can occur. Autumn (October to November) offers clear skies and crisp weather (15°C to 22°C), making it ideal for outdoor activities. Winter (December to February) is cold, with temperatures dropping below freezing at night, and snowfall in higher regions. Overall, Dalhousie is a year-round destination, each season offering a unique experience.
